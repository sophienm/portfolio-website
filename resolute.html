Keeping fit doesn't have to feel like a chore!

MY ROLE

Defining user flows, creating wireframes and the prototype, as well as developing the visual identity of the web application.

TOOLS

Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator

UNDERSTAND

THE CHALLENGE

Create the UI for the web application — based on the user research provided by CareerFoundry — that motivates people into an exercise routine that suits their level, schedule, and interests.

CONTEXT

Firstly, finding exercise routines for your level can be difficult, especially if you want to try something new. This responsive web app should help people get into an exercise of their choice by holding their hand a bit and providing routines, guides, interactive examples, and info.

Secondly, finding routines that fit into your schedule is not easy. The web app should encourage people who want to exercise to get into an easy routine for physical activities. This means fitting in as little as a 5-minute routine.

RESEARCH

As this project was focused on the user interface, the main research was already provided along with user stories and a persona — Rebecca.

USER FLOW

I completed the provided information by conducting a competitor analysis of applications providing a similar service. I focused my attention on user flows, imagery, layout, colour and typography. And with data from my research I started by creating user flows and first sketches.

MOODBOARD

The next step was to decide on the visual direction of the web application. To do so I created two moodboards based on what I discovered in my competitor analysis and the user persona I was provided.

Rebecca is a young, working mother who is looking for an engaging, fun and easy way to fit an exercise routine into her life.

For the following reasons I decided to continue with moodboard B:

1. Moodboard A has a masculine feel to it and considering the current topic of gender equality and identity I thought it best to choose a colour palette that is more inclusive. Moodboard B is more appropriate for a divers user base.

2. Moodboard B is fresh, cool and energetic due the yellow and green tone. Moodboard B is warm, sophisticated and strong, but too heavy. The web app is supposed to motivate the user to workout and I think that Moodboard A with its warm colour palette feels too comfortable and cozy to stimulate the user. The contrasting colours of Moodboard B have an energizing feel that motivates.

3. The app should inspire the user to become healthy and have fun while doing so. The green, turquoise and yellow of Moodboard B are associated with health, growth, nature, vitality and joy. And the colourful, neon palette inspired by the 80s athleisure wear is on point with the current trends.

WIREFRAMES

Working with a 12 column grid, applying visual design principles and putting together the style guide for Resolute. led to aesthetic changes which created consistency throughout the wireframes. In addition preference tests helped me solidify me design choices.

CONCLUSION

I thouroughly enjoyed creating the UI for this project. I challenged myself to work with bold, neon colours that pushed me out of my comfort zone. It was important to me to create a clean, minimalist visual identity, but keep the web application fun and light. Another challenge was to experiment with typography and not make the easy choice, but push myself to experiment more.
